Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

What should I wear when riding a bike?

Always wear a helmet to protect your head in case of an accident. Wear bright or reflective clothing to increase visibility to others. Consider wearing padded gloves and comfortable, weather-appropriate clothing.

How can I maintain my bike properly?

Regularly check your bike's tire pressure, brakes, and chain. Lubricate the chain to ensure smooth operation. Inspect your bike for any loose or damaged parts and fix them promptly. Keep your bike clean to prevent rust and wear.

What should I do if I get a flat tire while riding?

Carry a repair kit with you, including a spare tube, tire levers, and a pump. Find a safe place to stop and remove the wheel. Use the tire levers to remove the tire and replace the tube. Inflate the new tube and reassemble the wheel before continuing your ride.
